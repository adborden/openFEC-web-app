Enforcement matters

The FEC exclusively enforces civil federal campaign finance law. Possible violations of the law are identified one of four ways:

  1. The FEC regularly reviews committee reports and conducts audits.
  2. Anyone may file a sworn complaint with the FEC that a violation has occurred or is about to occur.
  3. Other government agencies may refer possible violations to the FEC.
  4. Any person or entity who believes it has committed a violation may self report to the Commission.

Enforcement programs

Matters Under Review (MURs)

MURs are standard enforcement cases. By law, the Commission must attempt to resolve MURs through a confidential investigative process. If that process fails to end with a conciliation agreement, either the Commission or the respondent(s) can choose to pursue the matter to court.
